Issue 01 · The soda problem

You can't change what you can't see.

Most people trying to fix a habit skip the only step that matters. They jump straight to willpower. Watch what happens when you don't.

Say someone wants to lose weight. Ask them why it isn't working and you get a shrug. "I don't know, I just can't." They are trying to fix a problem they have never actually looked at.

So before changing anything, they track. Just track. No shame, no rules. A week in, one number jumps off the screen: a liter of soda. Every single day. There it is. The thing hiding in plain sight.

That is the whole game. Track first, and the insight finds you.
Why this works (the science, fast)

Every habit runs the same four-step loop. Once you can see it, you can change it.

  • CueThe triggerSitting down to lunch.
  • CravingThe pullYou want the sugar hit and the fizz.
  • ResponseThe actionYou grab the soda.
  • RewardThe payoffQuick energy, and your brain files it to repeat.

You don't need to attack all four. You just need to see the loop clearly, then make one small change to one part of it.

Try this week

Drop one. Add one.

Make the bad one harder: don't keep soda in the house. If it isn't in the fridge, the loop never starts. (That's "make it invisible.")

Then add one good one that's easy to win: a ten-minute walk after lunch. Small enough that you'll actually do it. Track both.

A month from now you won't be guessing why anything is or isn't working. You'll have the map.
