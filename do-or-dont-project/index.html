Do or Don't

A habit tracker I designed and built from scratch: a radial map of what builds you and what corrodes you, scored and tracked over time.

Overview

Most habit trackers are guilt machines: break a streak and the app punishes you. Do or Don't takes a different view. It treats your days as data, plotting the actions that build you against the ones that corrode you, so you can actually see the shape of your life rather than chase a number.

I designed the concept, the interface, and built the whole thing myself, then designed a social campaign to launch it.

What I did

The work, broken down.

Design & build

  • Designed the radial wheel: 12 cool-toned facets for the things you DO, 13 warm orange-to-red facets for the things you DON'T.
  • Built four difficulty tiers into the dots, with hollow meaning committed and solid meaning completed.
  • Created week, month, and year views, with local persistence so your data stays yours.
  • Designed and built it for both desktop and mobile, plus a vertical social ad to launch it.
Outcome

A finished, living product I use myself, built to prove a point: the right interface can make self-tracking feel like seeing clearly instead of keeping score.

  • Fully working on desktop and mobile.
  • Designed, built, and shipped solo.
  • A matching vertical social campaign.
