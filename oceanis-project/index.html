Overview

Your next three years, in one view.

Oceanis models a few independent income streams against a simple spending plan and projects your net worth over the next three years: years, not decades. A compass reads on course or off course as the numbers move. Free to use, and your plan saves privately in your own browser. No account, nothing in the cloud.

Why now

A single paycheck is a single point of failure.

Single-employer, full-time work is giving way to remote, task-based, freelance income: the Upwork era. Diversifying across income streams is the new job security, and almost no tool is built for the people living it.

The paradigm

Not retirement. Freedom.

The old model: grind forty years, accumulate, stop at sixty-five. FIRE's twist (be frugal, invest, retire early) keeps the waiting and the retiring. Oceanis keeps the goal, freedom, and throws out the method. It isn't about saving harder or quitting early; it's about earning across more kinds of income until you reach your North Star, the number that funds your dream life, in a few years. Not retiring. No longer needing to work.

The engine

Three buckets of income.

01

Time for money.

A job or consulting. Reliable, but capped by your hours.

02

Product that scales.

Built once, sold many. Grows past your time.

03

Equity that compounds.

A stake, an asset, ownership that appreciates whether you show up or not.

Oceanis makes the shift visible: across the horizon the time-for-money line tapers while the scalable and equity lines climb. You watch yourself graduate from bucket one toward bucket three. Diversification, not frugality, is the engine.

The North Star

A ladder of freedom, not a retirement number.

Your goal isn't an abstract nest egg, it's a life. Oceanis turns it into a ladder of concrete milestones, each a step further from rent and closer to owning your time: from The Rig (a paid-off van, no landlord) up through a cabin, a tiny home, a homestead, an off-grid retreat, to the Blue Water Sailboat at the top. As your projected net worth climbs, the next rung comes into reach, so the goal always feels real, never abstract.

What I did

A simple plan you can steer by.

  • Defined the concept, brand, and financial model, then designed and built the working web app, in a day.
  • Built a live projection engine that turns income and spending assumptions into a real-time net-worth trajectory and a single on-course / off-course read.
  • Modeled independent income streams with editable growth curves (conservative or aggressive), logged actuals, and a categorized spending ("burn") plan.
  • Wrapped the boring-but-critical parts (taxes, credit repair, milestone goals) into modules that make a multi-year plan feel tangible and motivating.
  • Shipped it as a real, free tool: email login, private browser-saved plans, and a newsletter for people building from zero.
  • Verified the math independently, so every projected figure is correct, not just plausible.
