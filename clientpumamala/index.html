Functional Medicine  ·  Wellness Spa  ·  Private Residences

Puma Mala is where your home, your wellness practice, and your doctors share an address — a development in Ojochal designed for a way of living that doesn't yet exist here.

A single development that combines three things the region has never had in one place: a building of rentable medical offices, a membership-based wellness spa, and a collection of private residences whose owners live steps from both. The residences finance the vision. The wellness experience is what makes them unlike anything else on the market.

The three parts, one place

What lives inside Puma Mala

01

Functional Medicine

An upstairs floor of independent practitioners — general practice, dentistry, dermatology, nutrition, women's health, aesthetics — flowing from lighter cosmetic care at the front to clinical practice at the back, sharing reception, nursing, and scheduling.

02

The Wellness Spa

The ground-floor heart: café and hair studio at the entrance, gym and physical therapy beyond, sauna and cold plunge below. A membership sanctuary — evidence-based, therapeutic, built for everyday ritual.

03

The Residences

Luxury studios (from $229K) and one-bedrooms ($299–329K) with quartz kitchens, hardwood floors, and stone baths — the wellness center as a built-in amenity, plus the rare ability to rent on-site recovery suites with on-call nursing.

Why this is different

The paradigm shift

Developers build condos and hope a gym fills the brochure. Puma Mala inverts it: the wellness center and functional-medicine practice come first, and the residences are designed around them. An owner doesn't just buy a home — they buy proximity to care, a daily wellness practice, and a recovery-suite income stream that exists nowhere else in the region.

Comparable residences sell at similar prices with none of this integration, and local practitioners have already expressed interest in space. Nothing like it exists in the South Pacific zone — the model is proven nearby, but no one has brought it together with intention.

How it works

A deliberately simple model

Medical offices

Rented, not operated

Practitioners simply rent their space and share the nursing, reception, and scheduling infrastructure. No equipment to source, no networks to manage — clean, proven, and easy to say yes to.

The spa

Membership-based

A recurring-membership wellness club downstairs — predictable revenue, a real community, and a standard of practice the building can uphold.

The residences

Pre-sold to finance the build

A $500 reservation, then 25% at construction start, shell, utilities, and move-in — with financing options. The condos fund the vision that makes them valuable.

Recovery suites

A use, not an identity

Select ground-floor, accessibility-ready residences can be offered for short therapeutic-recovery stays with on-call nursing — surgery recovery, treatment travel, or rest. An optional income stream the region lacks — without the building ever becoming a care facility.

The flagship unit

Inside the residences

Double-height living rooms, sculptural staircases, and stone-and-hardwood interiors — designed to feel like the wellness sanctuary downstairs continues into your home.

Clarity of focus

What Puma Mala is not

—Not an assisted-living or retirement home. Recovery stays are one optional use of a few units, not the purpose.
—Not a doctor-subscription network or equipment-sourcing business — those are deferred as too complex to launch.
—Not "holistic" in the non-scientific sense. It is science-forward, evidence-based, and therapeutic.
—Not an insurance alternative or membership health-finance scheme.
—Not a sprawling multi-service clinic that staffs roles before the right people are found.
—Not a software platform — that vision exists, but later, and on its own track.
